# Homepage Prompt for Claude

Use this prompt to refine the current rebuilt homepage for The Base Movement without changing the existing design pattern.

## Prompt

> I am rebuilding the homepage for The Base Movement website.  
> I already have a design pattern, layout structure, spacing rhythm, and visual direction in place.  
> Do not redesign the page.  
> Do not suggest new sections unless absolutely necessary.  
> Do not change the visual pattern, component style, or overall layout logic.  
> Work only within the current homepage structure and improve the content, UX wording, hierarchy clarity, and credibility.  
>  
> Context:  
> This is a Ghana-focused political movement homepage with the central message “Ghana First, Jobs for the youth!”  
> The current rebuilt page already includes these homepage sections in this order:  
> 1. Header/navigation  
> 2. Hero section with headline, short supporting text, and two CTA buttons  
> 3. Two-card split section for Base Ghana and Base Diaspora  
> 4. “Our Foundation” section with three short principle/value blocks  
> 5. Stats section with large numbers  
> 6. “Latest updates” section with three content cards  
> 7. Final CTA/join section with a large headline, short paragraph, and CTA buttons  
> 8. Footer with links and newsletter signup  
>  
> I want you to refine this exact homepage, not replace it.  
>  
> Your task:  
> - Rewrite the copy for the current sections so the homepage feels more credible, political, clear, and public-facing.  
> - Keep the tone confident, hopeful, practical, and nation-building.  
> - Reduce vague language and replace it with more grounded, purposeful wording.  
> - Make the messaging stronger around youth jobs, national development, participation, accountability, and movement-building.  
> - Make the CTAs sharper and more persuasive.  
> - Improve trust by making stats, labels, and section wording feel more believable and specific.  
> - Keep everything concise and suitable for a modern movement homepage.  
>  
> Important constraints:  
> - Do not propose a redesign.  
> - Do not suggest new design patterns.  
> - Do not change the page structure.  
> - Do not give visual design advice unless it directly affects wording clarity.  
> - Keep the current section order.  
> - Preserve the headline “Ghana First, Jobs for the youth!” unless you give 3 slightly stronger alternatives that still stay very close to it.  
>  
> Specific guidance for each section:  
>  
> Hero:  
> - Keep the headline strong and campaign-ready.  
> - Rewrite the supporting paragraph so it sounds less generic and more politically grounded.  
> - Suggest better CTA text for the two existing buttons.  
>  
> Base Ghana / Base Diaspora:  
> - Keep this split.  
> - Rewrite both descriptions to clearly explain the difference between the two audiences.  
> - Make the buttons clearer and more action-oriented.  
>  
> Our Foundation:  
> - Keep the three-column structure.  
> - Rewrite the value blocks so they feel like real movement principles, not generic statements.  
> - Use language connected to jobs, discipline, service, accountability, growth, and national transformation.  
>  
> Stats:  
> - Recommend clearer labels for the existing numbers.  
> - Point out where the current stats may create trust issues if they are not explained.  
> - Give alternative wording patterns for stat labels and microcopy.  
>  
> Latest Updates:  
> - Keep the section.  
> - Improve the title, intro line if needed, and content card headline style.  
> - Make the update headlines feel more like meaningful movement or national development stories.  
>  
> Final CTA:  
> - Rewrite the headline and supporting paragraph to sound strong and believable.  
> - If the big number creates a credibility problem, suggest safer headline alternatives that keep the same energy without weakening the page.  
> - Improve the CTA button labels.  
>  
> Footer / newsletter:  
> - Improve the newsletter copy so it sounds worth subscribing to.  
> - Keep it short, serious, and useful.  
>  
> Output format:  
> 1. Section-by-section rewritten copy for the exact current homepage.  
> 2. Better CTA text options for each button.  
> 3. A “trust and clarity fixes” list focused only on wording and labels.  
> 4. A final version of the homepage copy ready to paste into the site.  
>  
> Writing style:  
> - Direct  
> - Concise  
> - Credible  
> - Politically aware  
> - Public-facing  
> - Not overly corporate  
> - Not poetic  
> - Not generic  
>  
> Avoid phrases like:  
> - empowering your journey  
> - unlocking potential  
> - building a better tomorrow  
> - transformative solutions  
> - all-in-one platform  
> - generic movement slogans without explanation  
>  
> Use the existing rebuilt homepage as the reference point and improve only the content and wording.  
>  
> Prioritize credibility over hype, and flag any number, phrase, or claim that may weaken public trust if left unexplained.
