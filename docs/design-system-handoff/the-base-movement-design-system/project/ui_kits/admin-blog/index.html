Lapaz registered 1,247 new patriots this weekend — here's how.

Branch 04 spent 12 hours canvassing Lapaz market, three taxi ranks and the trotro station. The numbers tell one story. The conversations tell a bigger one.

Kwesi Owusu Field coordinator · Greater Accra
Pub May 12 · 6 min read

The morning started slow. By 9 a.m. only six people had signed up at the market entrance — a normal trickle, not the river we had hoped for. Then Auntie Ama set up the speaker and asked one question that changed the day.

"They said they would create jobs. Where are the jobs? My son finished SHS three years ago. Tell me where the jobs are."

— Akua, kente trader · Lapaz market

By noon the line at our table stretched past the fishmongers. People did not want flyers. They wanted to be heard, and they wanted a number — not a slogan — that they could check back on.
