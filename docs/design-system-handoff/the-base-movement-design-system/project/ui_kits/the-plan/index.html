01
Core Pillar 01 · Economic responsibility

Transparent resource management for sustainable jobs

Ghana's mineral wealth must create jobs, not headlines. This pillar sets the rules, the fund, and the independent oversight body.

Ghana earned an estimated ₵14.8 billion from mineral royalties between 2020 and 2025. The number of youth in formal employment fell in the same period. This is the accountability gap The Plan addresses directly.

"Ring-fence 16% of annual mineral royalties into the Youth Employment Fund, administered by an independent board with published quarterly accounts."

The fund is not a new government agency. It is a legally binding trust, managed jointly by the National Development Planning Commission, three civil society seats, and one representative elected by The Base's member chapters.

How we hold ourselves to it.

Accountability is not a value. It is a mechanism. Here is ours.

01 · Public ledger

Every promise published

Every campaign commitment is recorded before any election with a named delivery officer and a measurable target.

02 · Monthly status

Live dashboard by constituency

Updated every 30 days. Green means on track. Red means the officer owes the public an explanation within 14 days.

03 · Member recall

Chapters can trigger a review

Any 500 verified members can petition for a full public review of a named promise. We cannot ignore it.

04 · Annual report

Audited, published, tabled

An independent auditor produces an annual report. We table it publicly — no edits, no omissions.
